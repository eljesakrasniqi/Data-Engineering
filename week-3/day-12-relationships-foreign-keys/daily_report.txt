What I understood better today:

* A primary key uniquely identifies each record in a table.
* A foreign key connects records between related tables.
* AUTOINCREMENT allows the database to generate unique IDs automatically.
* Relationships prevent duplicated and messy data.
* The order_items table is necessary to represent the many-to-many relationship between orders and products.
* INNER JOIN returns matching records from related tables.
* LEFT JOIN keeps all records from the left table, even when there is no match.
* Referential integrity protects the database from invalid relationships.
* Business reports become more accurate when data is stored in separate, well-connected tables.
