Day 14 - Daily Report

Today's goal was to design and build a complete relational database for a training center using SQLite. The project focused on creating a well-structured database that supports students, programs, instructors, enrollments, attendance, and payments while allowing management to generate business reports and perform data quality checks.

Tasks Completed:
- Planned the database structure before writing SQL.
- Created the database schema with six related tables:
  - students
  - programs
  - instructors
  - enrollments
  - attendance
  - payments
- Defined primary keys for every table.
- Added foreign keys to create relationships between tables.
- Applied database constraints including:
  - PRIMARY KEY
  - FOREIGN KEY
  - NOT NULL
  - UNIQUE
  - CHECK
- Inserted realistic sample data into every table.
- Added students from different cities.
- Added multiple programs and instructors.
- Created enrollments with active, completed, and dropped statuses.
- Added attendance records with both attended and missed sessions.
- Added payment records with paid, unpaid, and partial payment statuses.
- Tested foreign key relationships and validation rules.
- Created JOIN reports to combine information from multiple tables.
- Created aggregation reports using COUNT, SUM, AVG, GROUP BY, ORDER BY, and LIMIT.
- Created HAVING reports to filter grouped results.
- Performed data quality checks using LEFT JOIN to identify missing or inconsistent data.
- Wrote business insights based on the generated reports.
- Completed project documentation including README and database design explanation.

Reports Created:
- Student, program, and instructor reports.
- Active, completed, and dropped enrollment reports.
- Attendance reports.
- Payment reports.
- Student counts by city.
- Enrollment counts by status and program.
- Revenue reports by city and program.
- Attendance rate reports by student and program.
- Top students by attendance.
- Top programs by revenue.
- Instructor workload reports.
- Data quality reports for missing enrollments, payments, attendance, and other inconsistencies.

Data Quality Checks Performed:
- Students without enrollments.
- Programs without enrollments.
- Enrollments without payment records.
- Enrollments without attendance records.
- Active students with unpaid or partial payments.
- Students with low attendance but paid payments.
- Students with high attendance but unpaid or partial payments.
- Dropped students with payment records.
- Instructors without active students.
- Additional checks for incomplete or inconsistent records.

Skills Practiced:
- Relational Database Design
- Primary Keys
- Foreign Keys
- One-to-Many Relationships
- SQL Constraints
- SQLite
- INNER JOIN
- LEFT JOIN
- Aggregate Functions
- GROUP BY
- HAVING
- Business Reporting
- Data Quality Validation
- Database Documentation

Challenges:
- Designing the correct relationships between tables.
- Creating realistic test data that satisfies all business rules.
- Building queries that combine data from multiple related tables.
- Calculating attendance rates from attendance records.
- Creating reports that support business decision-making.

Outcome:
The database successfully supports student management, program management, attendance tracking, payment tracking, business reporting, and data quality validation. All required SQL files, reports, and documentation were completed successfully.
